# X (Twitter) Playbook

Working reference for X post copy and distribution, for both influencer creators and SEO/GEO content syndication. Figures below are reported values from the source skill (kostja94 marketing-skills); they reflect community-observed algorithm behavior, are subject to change, and have no guaranteed ranking effect. Treat them as starting hypotheses to test against your own post analytics, not fixed rules.

## Character and format specs

| Item | Value | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| Standard post | 280 chars | Default for non-Premium |
| Premium post | up to 25,000 chars | X Premium subscribers |
| URL | counts as ~23 chars | t.co shortens every link |
| Emoji | ~2 chars each | varies |
| Hashtags | 1-3 | more reads as spam |

Reported engagement sweet spots: general 71-100 chars; promotional 120-130; reply-driving questions 60-80.

## Algorithm signals (reported weights)

Replies dominate; likes are weak by comparison. Order of impact as reported:

| Signal | Reported effect | Practical move |
|--------|-----------------|----------------|
| Replies | strongest (cited ~54-75x a like) | end posts with an open question |
| Author replies in the chain | active reply chains cited ~75x visibility | reply to your own commenters fast |
| Bookmarks/saves | recommendation boost | write save-worthy posts (lists, refs) |
| Media (image/video) | image ~2x, video ~2-4x exposure | attach native media, not a link card |
| External links | cited ~-50% reach; regular accounts may see near-0% on link posts | keep links out of the main post |
| Posting volume | >10/day cited to cut later-post visibility ~80% | cap at ~5-8/day |

Avoid engagement bait ("RT if you agree"). Open questions, a clear point of view, and short stories are the reported high-reply formats.

## Link placement (the main SEO/GEO tactic)

External links suppress reach. To syndicate a blog post or landing page without paying the penalty:

- Put the link in a **reply** to your own post, not in the post body.
- Keep the value in the post itself so it earns engagement first.
- Hold links to roughly **1 per 5 posts** (reported ceiling).
- Premium / Premium+ accounts reportedly take a smaller link penalty than regular accounts; if X is a core channel, that tier may be worth it. Do not present it as required.

No proven Google-ranking effect from X posts. X traffic and indexed link previews may speed discovery of a page, but treat that as a distribution assist, not a ranking lever.

## TweepCred-style account health (reported thresholds)

| Condition | Reported effect |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Score < 65 | only your last ~3 posts surface in For You |
| New account | needs to build standing before posts appear broadly |
| Verified (Blue) | reported automatic standing bump |

Keep the account clean: no bulk operations, no spammy link dumps, consistent niche so clustering works in your favor.

## First-30-minute reach window

Posts are reportedly shown to a small group first; engagement in the first ~30 minutes decides whether reach expands. Practical implications:

- Post when your audience is active, then stay present to reply.
- Seed early replies and answer commenters quickly to feed the author-reply signal.
- Niche consistency helps the algorithm match your post to the right cluster.

## Thread structure

- 3-5 connected posts, numbered (1/5, 2/5...); threads reportedly extend impressions vs a single post.
- First post: strong hook, no literal "thread:" label.
- Each post stands alone (~80 chars) so it survives being reshared out of order.
- Last post: CTA, summary, or open question.
- Hook / value / CTA rough split: 10% / 70% / 20%.

## Content mix and media

- Suggested ratio: ~70% value (how-to, insight), ~20% interaction (polls, questions, AMA), ~10% promo.
- Video: short clips (~8-30s) reported at 2-4x text exposure; high completion rate cited to boost the score further.
- Link-preview cards (og:image, title ≤70 chars, description ≤200 chars, 1200×628 image) reportedly lift CTR. Set them on the destination page so the reply-link renders well.

## What an agent does here (keyless, Tier 1)

- Draft post/thread copy with live character counts against the limits above.
- Recommend link-in-reply structure and flag any draft that puts a link in the body.
- Read the user's **own** post analytics (pasted in or from their X dashboard) to check reply rate, first-30-min engagement, and link-post reach against these reported benchmarks.
- Inspect **public** posts/profiles the user names to model cadence and format. No paid API required.
