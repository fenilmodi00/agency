# TikTok Platform Playbook

How to write TikTok captions and plan short-form video so the post earns watch-time on muted, fast-scrolling feeds. Creator-content focused. All tactics work from the user's own TikTok analytics and the public caption/specs you can see on any post — no paid tool required.

## Why captions carry the post

Most viewers watch with sound off, so on-screen text and the written caption do the work the audio can't. The OSS source cites a 12-20% watch-time lift with captions and "80%+ watch without sound"; treat these as directional industry figures, not guarantees, and verify against your own retention graph in TikTok analytics. Practical takeaway: write for the muted viewer first.

## Caption structure

| Part | Length | Job |
|------|--------|-----|
| Hook | 5-7 words | Stop the scroll; open a curiosity gap |
| Context | 1-2 sentences | Add what the video can't show: price, size, backstory, the catch |
| CTA | optional, 1 line | "Save this," "Link in bio," "Part 2 coming" |

Hook patterns that work: name the audience ("Stop scrolling if you sell on Etsy"), show effort/stakes ("This took me 47 attempts"), or promise a payoff ("Wait for the final result"). Lead with the hook — TikTok truncates captions, so the first words matter most.

## Video specs

| Spec | Value | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| Aspect ratio | 9:16 portrait | Fills the mobile screen |
| Resolution | 1080x1920 px | Recommended |
| Container/codec | MP4 or MOV, H.264 video, AAC audio | Standard upload format |
| Length | 3 sec to 10 min | See length bands below |
| File size | varies by device (roughly tens of MB on mobile) | Confirm the current cap in-app before a large upload |

## Length bands

| Length | Best for |
|--------|----------|
| 15-60 sec | Quick tips, humor, single-hook posts |
| 1-3 min | Tutorials, storytelling |
| 3-10 min | Deep dives, multi-step walkthroughs |

## Hook and retention mechanics

- **First 1-2 seconds decide it.** State the payoff or the conflict immediately; don't open with a slow logo or "hey guys."
- **Open a loop, close it later.** Promise a result or reveal in the hook, deliver near the end — this is what pulls watchers past the drop-off point.
- **Re-hook mid-video** on longer cuts (a new angle, a "but here's the problem") to reset attention before the audience leaves.
- **Loop the ending into the beginning** so the video replays cleanly; replays count as watch-time.
- Read your own retention curve in TikTok analytics and find the second where viewers drop. That timestamp is what to fix next, not a guess.

## On-screen text and accessibility

- **High contrast.** Use a strong font-against-background contrast so text is legible on a phone in sunlight; the source notes ~75% watch on mute.
- **Auto-captions, then edit.** TikTok's built-in captions add accessibility and feed search keywords — turn them on, but proofread, since auto-transcription mangles names and jargon.
- **Keep text inside safe margins.** UI elements (username, buttons) cover the bottom and right edges; keep key words clear of them.
- **Put searchable keywords in the caption and on-screen text.** TikTok indexes both for in-app search. This helps discovery; it is not a confirmed ranking signal, so don't keyword-stuff.

## Publish-ready output checklist

When generating a TikTok post, hand back:

1. Caption — hook + context + optional CTA, hook first.
2. Video script or shot list (if a video brief is in scope), with the hook line called out.
3. Specs — 9:16, 1080x1920, target length band.
4. Hashtags — 3-5, niche-relevant over broad/generic.
